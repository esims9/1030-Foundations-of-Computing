Bouldering

Bouldering in Utah

Bouldering is a very popular sport in Utah. It is similar to rock climbing, but there are no ropes. With only some chalk and your hands, the object is to scale the rock. The routes for bouldering are much shorter than rock climbing routes, but they are no less difficult. Utah is known for it's bouldering and rock climbing community. Multiple gyms and outdoor areas offer bouldering. Here is a list of popular outdoor bouldering areas and the number of problems, or routes, they have:

Moe's Valley, St. George 47 Problems
Big Bend Bouldering Area, Moab 73 Problems
Joe's Valley, Orangeville 368 Problems
American Fork Canyon, American Fork 36 Problems

Bouldering Gear

There is not a lot of gear that you need to start bouldering. The only items needed are climbing shoes, an optional crash pad, chalk, and a chalk bag to hold the chalk. The climbing shoes are specifically made to help your feet to find purchase upon the rock wall. A chalk bag is used to keep your hands dry from sweat so you don't slip when grabbing a hold. A crash pad is placed beneath you as you climb upon a rock wall outside. If you find a climbing gym that has bouldering, the floor is one large crash pad so you don't need to supply one yourself. There are other kinds of gear you can acquire that help with bouldering, but those three items are perfect for beginners.

  • Climbing shoes
  • Chalk
  • Chalk bag
  • Crash pad
  • Climbing brush
